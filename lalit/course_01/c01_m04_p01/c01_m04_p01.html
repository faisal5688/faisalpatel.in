Exercise

In your career — and your life — thus far, you have likely encountered situations when your values conflicted with what you were asked to do. Often it is not easy to align your own personal values and purpose with those of your boss, your co-workers, your direct reports, your customers/clients, or your firm at work (and of course, with others in life more generally). This exercise is designed to help you identify and develop the competencies necessary to achieve that alignment.

Complete this worksheet for your tale of two stories examples. You can also download this worksheet and save it for future reference by clicking the Create PDF button at the end of this worksheet.

Objectives:

  1. To reflect on your previous experiences, successful and less so, at effectively voicing and acting on your values.
  2. To discover which conditions and problem definitions empower you to effectively voice your values, and which tend to inhibit that action.
Q1) Part I: Recall a time in your work experience when your values conflicted with what you were expected to do in a particular, non-trivial decision, and you spoke up and acted to resolve the conflict. [If you are an educator, you can think of a classroom or academic institution experience; if you are a learner in another non-work context, you can think of a more general life experience.]

What did you do, and what was the impact?
Q2) Part I: Based on your answer to Question 1, what motivated you to speak up and act?
Q3) Part I: Based on your answer to Question 1, how satisfied are you? How would you like to have responded? (This question is not about rejecting or defending past actions but rather imagining your Ideal Scenario.)
Q4) Part I: Based on your answer to Question 1, what things made it easier for you to speak/act (the “Enablers”)? (both things within your own control and things within the control of others)
Q5) Part 1: Based on your answer to Question 1, what things made it harder for you to speak/act (the “Disablers”)? (both things within your control and things within the control of others)
Q6) Part II: Recall a time in your work experience when your values conflicted with what you were expected to do in a particular, non-trivial decision, and you did not speak up or act to resolve the conflict. [If you are an educator, you can think of a classroom or academic institution experience; if you are a learner in another non-work context, you can think of a more general life experience.]

What did you do, and what was the impact?
Q7) Part II: Based on your answer to Question 6, why didn't you speak up or act? What would have motivated you to speak up or act?
Q8) Part II: Based on your answer to Question 6, how satisfied are you? How would you like to have responded? (This question is not about rejecting or defending past actions but rather imagining your Ideal Scenario.)
Q9) Part II: Based on your answer to Question 6, what things would have made it easier for you to speak/act (the “Enablers”)? (both things within your control and things within the control of others)
Q10) Part II: Based on your answer to Question 6, what things made it harder for you to speak/act (the “Disablers”)? (both things within your control and things within the control of others)
