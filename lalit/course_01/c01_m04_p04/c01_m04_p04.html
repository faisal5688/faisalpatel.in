REASONS AND RATIONALIZATION
It is not uncommon to experience a values conflict, meaning a situation with an ethical component that makes it hard to act on your values.
We often view these values conflicts as a disruption to our day-to-day.
But you will encounter these conflicts throughout your career.
When we think of these issues as not normal they can seem overwhelming.
What if you anticipate the types of conflicts you are likely to encounter?
Would you be better prepared to resolve them?
Would it change the way you see the other person?
