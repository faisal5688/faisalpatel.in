Why people choose not to speak up

According to the Ethics and Compliance Initiative (ECI), 1 in 4 people surveyed around the world say they are aware of misconduct in the workplace and yet they have not spoken up about it.

Small acts of unethical behavior can lead to more and larger acts - this is the slippery slope described in the Science of
(Dis)Honesty course. What makes it hard to speak up to address and resolve ethical issues when they arises? There are four common reasons people choose not to speak up.

Review the four reasons and then flip each card to revisit how these reasons may have been a factor in some of the stories you encountered in previous Ethics & Integrity courses.
